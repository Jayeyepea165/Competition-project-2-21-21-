Virtual Shopping: Here's how you can try clothes on online

For the past couple of years, the stores on high streets across The US have been increasingly threatened because of high rents and online shopping â€“ a problem made worse by the pandemic.

In the wake of this change, companies have started to look for more appealing ways to attract customers online.

Style.me is one of the companies that has benefited from this increase in interest.

It creates a plugin for websites that allows customers to create online avatars to try on clothes for them, similarly to Cher in Clueless.

Customers can import and make their avatars look like themselves by importing a full body picture of themsleves with options to change skin tone and hairstyles.

The website remembers the avatar a customer creates so they don't have to design it for every outfit they want to 'try on.'
